Original Lifecycle

The main hindrance I found in my original lifecycle was in scope. Early on it was easy to build self contained features that could be implemented but as the project moved on, implementing a new feature often involved breaking or changing something I had previously built. To carry on with my development I found I had to take into account dependencies and often revisit previous work to ensure stability in my project.

Another issue with my lifecycle was attempting to build API and client side at the same time, this was often confusing and slowed down progress. I discovered that if I could build and test the API independently of the JS + HTML implementation I could make quicker progress and clearer progress. Below is the life cycle I believe I utilised the most, demonstrating how I split build and how dependencies in design often led me to revisit previous builds.

Schedule

My final schedule defines the final builds/increments my project actually took, some have been redefined from my initial schedule as I found my initial schedule to be too sparse and not accurately covering the required builds I required. This is an accurate representation of the progress I made and what I was focused on developing at any one time.

Although I did not get enough time to fully complete the extra functionalities that I wished this was not due to scheduling but rather can be attributed to inexperience web development causing quality issues preventing progress and problems with my design.

When I started the project I initially found I was making rapid progress and as I was ahead of schedule I redefined the scope of my project to include many extra features. However this early progress although functional was not flexible or easy to build upon. As such I ended up having to rebuild my API, AJAX and Database model to reflect a better quality design that could be utilised for the features I had planned on developing. The time lost in this redesign was too much to recover from to develop all the planned features.

Reflection

If I was to attempt this project again, I would firstly spend much longer planning the intricacies of my design. I lost a lot of time because I developed without truly thinking of the impact my decisions would have on the project as a whole.

Secondly, I would be wary of expanding scope, I would rather have a smaller excellent complete product then a bigger but less excellent one.

Finally, I would have developed the API in earlier and larger stages and left the client side implementation for later stages as I believe that would allow for more holistic and time efficient development.
